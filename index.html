Research Interests

Inspired by my musical experiences as a composer, my research focuses on designing generative models and interfaces to support music making, and more generally the creative process. I am interested in expanding the creative reach of both novices and experienced artists, and working at the intersection of machine learning, human-computer interaction, music composition / improvisation, creativity and cognition.

My research has included:
  • Generative Models: [Music Transformer]  [Coconet]  [Wave2Midi2Wave]  [Infilling Piano Performances]  [Transformer-NADE] 
  • Controllable Performance / Audio Synthesis: [MIDI DDSP]  [Synthesizers] 
  • Human-AI Co-Creation: [AI Song Contest]  [Cococo]  [Bach Doodle]  [ChordRipple] 
  • Human-in-the-Loop ML: [Active Learning of Intuitive Control Knobs for Synthesizers Using Gaussian Processes] 
  • Visualization: [Music Transformer] 
  • Evaluation: [Expressive Communication: A Common Framework for Evaluating Developments in Generative Models and Steering Interfaces]  [AI Song Contest] 
  • In the Wild: [AI Song Contest]  [Coconet powered Google’s first AI Doodle (the Bach Doodle), in two days enabling tens of millions of users around the world to co-compose with ML in their browser] 
  • Music across Cultures: [Melodic Variations: Toward Cross-Cultural Transformation] 
I envision designing machine learning (ML) algorithms in tandem with human-computer interactions (HCI), to develop people-centric creative applications.
  • HCI enables us to identify new creative tasks, and design new user interface environments and interactions that can be both helpful for people in achieving their creative goals and helpful for models to learn from users’ behavior and adapt to users needs.
  • New human-in-the-loop ML algorithms can in turn enable new interaction possibilities and offer new workflows for creativity.
Below are some research directions I am excited about. Also open to new ideas and topics.
  • Learning from human feedback to calibrate generative models to how we experience the ups and downs, twists and turns in music, in order to steer generative models to support storytelling.
  • Designing adaptive artist-in-the-loop (reinforcement) learning algorithms to support artistic practice and push the boundary of art.
  • Leveraging generative models to design new musical interactions, instruments, and games that engage and uplevel novices (and also artists) in music making.
  • Designing visualizations of music, model behaviors and user behaviors to better understand how we can leverage ML for creative (mis)use.
  • Designing technology to better support creative practices from different cultural traditions (inspired by the expressiveness of pitch bends when playing the guzheng, and the challenges of writing lyrics in a tonal language such as Cantonese).
Prospective postdocs and students

I am very honored to receive a Canada CIFAR AI Chair, and am recruiting postdocs, PhD students, master students, and visiting students / interns through Mila.

Prospective PhD and master students in Computer Science (to start Fall 2023):

  1. You are highly recommended to apply through Mila's Request for Supervision program by December 1st, in addition to University of Montreal by Feburary 1st (see below).

  4. Apply through University of Montreal and choose the Mila program, by February 1st . Tips: The graduate research program is conducted in English, even though the online application form is in French.
